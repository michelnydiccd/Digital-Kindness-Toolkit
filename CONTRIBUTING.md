# Contributing to Digital Kindness Toolkit

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the Digital Kindness Toolkit! This project thrives on community collaboration, and we welcome contributions from everyone.

## How Can I Contribute?

### 1. **Suggest Resources**
- Share links to Philippine‑based hotlines, legal aid services, mental health support, or educational materials.
- Add resources by creating a new issue with the label `resource` or by submitting a pull request that adds them to the appropriate directory.

### 2. **Share Your Story**
- You can share your personal experience with online harassment anonymously or with attribution.
- Stories help others feel less alone and provide real‑life examples of resilience.
- Create an issue with the label `story`, or submit a pull request adding your story to the `/stories` directory.

### 3. **Propose Campaigns**
- Have an idea for a social‑media campaign, a workshop, or an advocacy initiative? We'd love to hear it!
- Open an issue with the label `campaign` and describe your idea.

### 4. **Improve Content**
- Fix typos, improve phrasing, translate content into Filipino or other Philippine languages, or reorganize information for clarity.
- Simply fork the repository, make your changes, and open a pull request.

### 5. **Spread the Word**
- Share the project on your social media, blogs, or community groups.
- Encourage others to contribute.

## Contribution Workflow

1. **Find an Issue** – Browse open issues labeled `good first issue`, `resource`, `story`, or `campaign`. If you have a new idea, open a new issue first.
2. **Fork the Repository** – Click the "Fork" button at the top right of this page.
3. **Create a Branch** – Create a new branch for your changes (e.g., `add‑hotline‑list`).
4. **Make Your Changes** – Edit files, add new content, or fix bugs.
5. **Commit Your Changes** – Write a clear commit message describing what you've done.
6. **Open a Pull Request** – Go to your fork and click "New pull request".
   - Reference the related issue (e.g., `Closes #1`).
   - Describe your changes and why they are valuable.
7. **Review & Merge** – Wait for maintainers or community members to review your PR. Once approved, it will be merged!

## Code of Conduct

Please read our [Code of Conduct](code_of_conduct.md) before participating. We are committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and harassment‑free environment for everyone.

## Need Help?

If you're new to GitHub or open‑source contributions, don't hesitate to ask! You can:
- Comment on the issue you're interested in.
- Reach out via social media (if the maintainer has provided contact info).
- Check out GitHub's own [guide to contributing](https://docs.github.com/en/get-started/quickstart/contributing-to-projects).
